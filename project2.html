user-enrollment flow

This is the product of of UX research and usability testing I conducted for the Desafío Latam website. The end result is a research-based, user tested, interactive prototype

Desafío Latam is an IT-development bootcamp I enrolled at. The task was to look at the site with a critical eye and identify user pain points regarding the goal of conversion

After user testing, the insights and conclusions obtained during the research stages were applied to an interactive prototype to facilitate further testing.


Here's a rough outline of how I did this:

  • The most important step was to map out the flow of our user's actions. This is crucial to ensure a seamless sequence of screens, and efficient component building: it shows what needs to be there and what doesn't, and how evertything works within a single system

  • Because Desafío Latam's brand is well established, I needed to take samples to start crafting my UI Kit, the first image shows the typography and color palette guides. I modified sizes, and added color shades to accomodate for more instances (such as "focus", and lower opacity to use when needed)

  • I then proceeded to create all components I would need. Taking cues from what was already built in the live website. This included anything from buttons, text inputs, icons, content cards, a new navigation bar and a footer component.
